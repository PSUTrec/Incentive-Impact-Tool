The Electric Vehicle Incentive Cost and Impact Tool

This tool is currently in beta. Please report bugs and errors to TREC.

Hover over items to learn more about what they do.

The Electric Vehicle Incentive Cost and Impact Tool was developed to give public stakeholders and advocates the ability to visualize the potential outcomes of an electric vehicle incentive program. Specifically, it estimates the cost efficiency of a proposed program, that is, the cost per kg CO2 avoided by each mode over the course of one year. It also takes proposed budget into consideration to calculate the potential number of incentives made available, by mode, and the amount of total CO2 avoided from ICE VMT displacement, by mode. These results are provided both in the case where the entire budget is spent on a single mode and the case where the budget is distributed among several modes.

The tool is highly versatile and allows users to understand impacts in any region of interest. It does this by accepting information gathered locally about current internal combustion engine (ICE) automobile trip making, electricity generation emissions profiles, and the average fuel economies of e-vehicles. Several state- and nationwide-level presets are available for quick use; however, this information can also be entered manually if more localized data exist.

Finally, the incentive program designed by the user can be exported in a report form, which can be used for quick reference when discussing incentive program design with other stakeholders.
